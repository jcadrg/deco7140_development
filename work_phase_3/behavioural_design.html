Behavioural Design

The focus of this section of the Style Guide is ensuring user needs for usability and function are supported.

Link Styles

Our links are designed for clarity, accessibility, and recognizability across all devices. They guide users through actions without overwhelming them, and maintain visibility across light and dark backgrounds.

Normal Link Hover State (hover me) Focus State (tab to me) Disabled Link

Each state (normal, hover, focus, disabled) supports user expectations and accessibility. Links meet WCAG 1.4.3 (contrast), 2.4.4 (link purpose), and 2.1.1 (keyboard access). Focus rings help keyboard users, while hover effects aid mouse navigation. Disabled links are visually distinct and non-interactive.

Menus and Navigation

The navigation menu reflects the sites information architecture and supports usability through clear labels and grouping. It ensures users can move efficiently through the platform, with consideration for screen readers and keyboard users.

This screenshot displays a horizontal navigation bar with clear section labels and hover states. It emphasizes visual grouping, supporting recognition over recall, and aligns with WCAG guidelines for accessibility.

Card Components

Cards help organize content into manageable, bite-sized sections. They summarize key data and provide entry points to deeper engagement or detailed views. We use them to present hobby projects, event listings, or community posts in a clean, digestible format.

[ Insert card layout screenshot here ]

The mock-up will demonstrate consistent spacing, clear hierarchy (title, body, CTA), and optional metadata like time or tags. Cards are styled to visually group related content while remaining accessible with screen readers and keyboard navigation.

Icons

Icons enhance quick recognition. They are paired with text or include aria-labels for accessibility.

Icons follow strong signifier principles. Where used alone, they are supplemented with accessible labels for WCAG 1.1.1 compliance.
